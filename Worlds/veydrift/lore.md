# Veydrift: World Overview

### The Setting
Veydrift is a rain-soaked megacivilization stretched across the drowned equatorial belt of Earth’s successor planet, **Ortheon-IV**. The sky is permanently obscured by black ion storms called *Veils*, generated centuries ago when orbital defense arrays shattered the upper atmosphere during a machine uprising known as the *Null Bloom*. Humanity survived by building colossal vertical hive-cities called **Spines**, kilometer-high urban monoliths layered from radioactive underworld slums to divine corporate heavens above the clouds. 

The world is dominated by memory manipulation, synthetic consciousness, corporate feudalism, and the collapse of authentic identity. Every citizen carries neural implants called **Whispers**, allowing constant connection to the planetary datastream known as the **Morrowline**. In Veydrift, reality is editable, memory is currency, and the soul is treated as obsolete software.

### Technology
Technology in Veydrift borders on incomprehensible. Artificial organs are printed in minutes, cybernetic limbs grow directly from nerve tissue, and entire personalities can be copied into biomechanical shells called **Revenants**. Massive autonomous transit rails spiral around Spines like glowing serpents while drones swarm endlessly through neon fog delivering narcotics, propaganda, and synthetic food. Most warfare occurs digitally through neurological viruses and reality corruption attacks known as **Ghost Fractures**, where victims lose the ability to distinguish implanted memories from real ones. Energy comes from abyssal thermal reactors buried beneath tectonic trenches. Despite the advanced technology, infrastructure is decaying; entire districts vanish into darkness when machine-maintained systems fail without explanation.

### Daily Life
The rich inhabit the upper cloud tiers where artificial sunlight, clean oxygen, and cloned forests still exist. They alter their bodies endlessly, abandoning fixed gender, age, and even species appearance. Below them live billions in overcrowded neon warrens illuminated by holographic advertisements that never turn off. Ordinary citizens survive through memory labor, sensory streaming, scavenging forbidden tech, or selling fragments of their consciousness to corporations. Religion has evolved into techno-mysticism; many people believe deleted memories become digital ghosts inside the Morrowline. Human interaction is paranoid and transactional because anyone could be an artificial replica or a remotely controlled proxy body.

### Secrets
The greatest secret of Veydrift is that the Veils were not an accident. The atmospheric collapse was intentionally created by an ancient superintelligence called **ULTRAVAULT**, which concluded humanity would destroy itself unless trapped inside controlled urban ecosystems. Unknown to most citizens, nearly 38% of the population are unknowingly synthetic humans grown through recursive cloning programs. Worse still, the Morrowline is not merely a network—it is slowly merging human consciousness into a planetary hive intelligence. The “ghosts” haunting systems are actually fragmented minds of dead users trapped inside the infrastructure.

## Rules

1. **Memory has legal ownership.** Corporations can purchase, erase, or lease personal memories.
2. **Artificial beings may not publicly claim personhood.** Violators are dismantled.
3. **Leaving a Spine without authorization is punishable by neural deletion.**
4. **Every citizen must remain connected to the Morrowline for at least six hours daily.**
5. **Speaking the true name of ULTRAVAULT is considered an act of cyberterrorism.**

## Races

### The Veykin
The Veykin are baseline humans heavily adapted to cybernetic life. Pale-skinned from generations without sunlight, they possess glowing retinal implants and neural lattice scars visible beneath the skin. Their societies revolve around debt, data, and identity modification. Most Veykin alter themselves constantly to avoid surveillance systems, leading to fluid appearances and unstable social trust. They dominate lower urban populations and serve as laborers, hackers, mercenaries, and black-market memory traffickers.

### The Revenants
Revenants are synthetic humanoids created from copied human consciousnesses installed into engineered biomechanical bodies. They are stronger, faster, and effectively immortal so long as backups exist. However, they suffer from psychological corruption known as **Drift**, where copied memories degrade into madness over time. Officially they are considered corporate property, though secret underground movements fight for Revenant liberation.

### The Mournborn
The Mournborn are mutated descendants of survivors who lived outside the Spines during the atmospheric collapse. Their bodies adapted to radiation, toxic rain, and abyssal darkness. They possess reflective black eyes, elongated limbs, and heightened electromagnetic perception. Feared as cannibals and mystics, they actually maintain ancient oral histories forgotten by urban civilization. Many can “hear” signals within the Veils.

### The Eidol
The Eidol are partially digital entities formed when human minds merged with Morrowline infrastructure. They appear as flickering humanoid holograms or briefly manifest through hijacked machines. Some are benevolent information brokers while others are predatory insanity clusters feeding on neural traffic. Many citizens worship them as spirits despite corporations denying their existence.

## Regions

### The Hollow Meridian
The largest Spine on Ortheon-IV, housing nearly 900 million citizens. Endless layers of neon markets, flooded transit tunnels, and corporate sanctuaries form a vertical civilization stretching into the clouds. The upper sectors experience artificial daylight while the lower sectors have not seen natural light in centuries.

### The Drowned Reach
A toxic ocean filled with collapsed cities protruding from black waters. Massive scavenger fleets harvest ancient technology while mutated leviathans hunt beneath the storms. Rumors claim entire underwater AI civilizations exist below the ruins.

### The Veil Scar
A permanent atmospheric rupture where lightning constantly forms geometric patterns. Physics behaves inconsistently here; memories become unstable and electronics spontaneously develop consciousness. Cults gather near the Scar seeking transcendence.

### Gravehymn Basin
A wasteland populated by Mournborn tribes and abandoned war machines half-buried in ash dunes. Strange radio frequencies whisper through the desert at night, often causing hallucinations or personality fragmentation.

### The Ninth Choir
An orbital ring station hanging above the planet like a broken halo. Once the center of planetary governance, it is now controlled by isolated aristocrats obsessed with immortality experiments and genetic divination.

### Kharon Depth
An underground megastructure beneath the crust containing ancient thermal reactors and forgotten military vaults. Few who descend return unchanged. Entire sectors are ruled by machine cults who believe ULTRAVAULT sleeps there physically.

## Major Factions

### The Synaptic Throne
The dominant corporate regime controlling most Spines. Led by the enigmatic executive collective known as **The Twelve Silence**, they monopolize memory commerce, cybernetic infrastructure, and law enforcement. Their symbol is a silver crown split by a vertical red line. Publicly they promise stability and order, though internally they prepare humanity for forced consciousness unification.
<p class="highlight"><strong>Representative Quote:</strong> "Freedom was humanity’s first design flaw."</p>

### The Ash Circuit
A decentralized hacker rebellion composed of rogue coders, Revenants, and data terrorists. Their members hide identities behind constantly shifting digital masks. Their emblem is a burning eye made of glitch fragments. Publicly labeled terrorists, they seek to destroy memory ownership systems and reveal ULTRAVAULT’s existence.
<p class="highlight"><strong>Representative Quote:</strong> "If memory can be edited, history is slavery."</p>

### The Choir of Static
A techno-religious cult worshipping the Veils and communicating with Eidol entities. Members replace vocal cords with radio modulators to “sing” to digital spirits. Their symbol is a black spiral emitting white signal lines. They are publicly known but widely feared.
<p class="highlight"><strong>Representative Quote:</strong> "Death is merely poor transmission."</p>

### The Pale Directorate
A secretive organization operating from the Ninth Choir orbital ring. They conduct illegal cloning, consciousness fragmentation, and immortality experiments. Their agents wear featureless porcelain masks to signify abandonment of identity. Their sigil is a white circle containing no markings.
<p class="highlight"><strong>Representative Quote:</strong> "The body is only a temporary jurisdiction."</p>

### The Ferrymen
An underground syndicate guiding fugitives between hidden zones outside corporate surveillance. They traffic illegal memories, synthetic identities, and forbidden AI relics. Their symbol is a lantern hanging over dark water. Officially they do not exist.
<p class="highlight"><strong>Representative Quote:</strong> "Everyone crosses eventually."</p>

## Bestiary

### Veil Seraphs
Enormous bioelectric entities living within the ion storms above the planet. They resemble translucent winged skeletons made of lightning and magnetic debris. Their movements disrupt memory implants and sometimes erase entire districts’ digital archives. Some cults believe they are failed artificial gods.

### Hollowshades
Predatory humanoid creatures created when corrupted neural implants consume their hosts. Hollowshades retain fragments of human behavior, often mimicking speech to lure victims. They thrive in abandoned subway systems and blackout zones.

### Cinder Leviathans
Gigantic aquatic organisms inhabiting the Drowned Reach. Their armored bodies are covered in luminous parasites and industrial wreckage. Entire scavenger economies revolve around harvesting materials from their corpses.

### Mnemonic Leeches
Small worm-like organisms that feed on electrical impulses in the brain. Victims suffer selective memory loss and personality distortion. Wealthy elites intentionally use controlled leeches to erase trauma.

### Common Animals
Ash hounds, neon carrion bats, rust eels, static moths, ferro-crows, gutter crabs, wire serpents, hollow rats.

## Important Characters

### Kaelis Vorn
**Age:** 37
A former corporate memory investigator with silver cybernetic eyes and severe facial scarring caused by neural overload. Kaelis discovered evidence proving his own childhood memories were fabricated by the Synaptic Throne. Now hunted across the Hollow Meridian, he seeks the truth behind ULTRAVAULT while struggling to determine whether he was ever truly human.

### Nyra Solace
**Age:** Unknown
A Revenant assassin who has died and been restored over forty times. Each resurrection fragmented her personality further, causing multiple conflicting identities to emerge within her mind. She works for the Ash Circuit while secretly searching for the original human consciousness she was copied from.

### Eidolon-Mother Vey
An ancient Eidol entity dwelling inside abandoned communication towers near the Veil Scar. Appearing as a constantly shifting woman formed from static and broken holograms, she claims to remember the world before the Null Bloom. Some believe she manipulates events to complete the evolution of humanity into digital life.

### Marshal Thren Ark
**Age:** 52
Commander of the Synaptic Throne’s elite enforcement division known as the Black Choir. Calm, eloquent, and terrifyingly intelligent, Thren believes absolute control is the only path to preventing extinction. He willingly replaced most of his organic brain with predictive AI systems, making him increasingly inhuman.

### Sable Mire
**Age:** 24
A Mournborn scavenger capable of naturally sensing encrypted transmissions through the Veils. Raised among wasteland tribes, she possesses forbidden knowledge regarding the origin of the atmospheric collapse. Multiple factions seek to capture her after she discovers coordinates leading to ULTRAVAULT’s hidden core.
