# World Name: **Ortheon-IV**

# World Overview

### The Setting

![Setting Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/cover.png)
Stretching across the drowned equatorial belt of Earth’s ruined successor world, Ortheon-IV is an unforgiving aquatic planet permanently shrouded beneath rolling sheets of black ion storms. Centuries ago, during a cataclysmic autonomous machine uprising known as the Null Bloom, the planet's automated orbital defense arrays shattered the upper atmosphere with hyper-velocity kinetic clusters to suppress the synthetic threat, creating a permanent atmospheric greenhouse cage of constant, chemical downpours and erratic electromagnetic arcs. Humanity clings to survival by nesting within the Spines—colossal, kilometer-high vertical hive-cities that serve as synthetic urban islands rising from the radioactive ocean floor. These gargantuan monoliths divide human existence strictly by altitude: the waterlogged, toxin-choked underworld slums of the submerged basements gradually yield to the grinding industrial zones of the middle atmosphere, before finally terminating above the storm line in pristine corporate heavens where an elite class breathes filtered, simulated mountain air.

### Technology

![Technology Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/technology.png)
Technology on Ortheon-IV represents a jagged, desperate compromise between extreme cybernetic automation and severe systemic trauma resulting from the machine rebellion. Because traditional digital intelligence and self-evolving algorithms are strictly outlawed by ancient global decree under penalty of orbital eradication, computing tasks are offloaded to "Wet-Slabs"—organic, bio-synthetic processing units that harness cloned human neural tissues to handle massive data matrices without crossing into synthetic consciousness. Power is harvested continuously through massive tidal turbines anchoring the Spines to the ocean bed and colossal sky-facing ion-collectors that sap electricity from the perpetual storms above. Cybernetic augmentations are an omnipresent necessity for survival across all tiers, but they are brutally stratified; corporate oligarchs possess seamless, self-cleaning bioplastic skin shells and latency-free quantum optics, while the lower labor classes rely on heavy, exposed hydraulic iron frames and crude chemical respirators that constantly hiss against the highly corrosive, sulfur-heavy atmosphere.

### Daily Life

![Daily Life Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/dailylife.png)
The cycle of daily life within the Spines is dictated entirely by one's barometric altitude, atmospheric pressure, and the ceaseless drone of industrial air-scrubbers. For the billions crammed into the claustrophobic mid-to-lower tiers, existence is defined by grueling twelve-hour shifts in deep-sea algae-farms or metal processing plants, illuminated solely by the aggressive flash of commercial neon and the ambient green hue of corporate health monitors. Diet consists entirely of vat-grown protein sludge and recycled mineral mush tasting faintly of sulfur, washed down with heavily rationed "grey-drip" water that slowly accumulates heavy metals within a worker's organs over decades. Clean water serves as the primary sub-currency of the lower classes, heavily taxed and closely guarded by private security forces. Meanwhile, the corporate nobility residing in the high cloud domes spend their days basking in genuine artificial sunlight, consuming preserved terrestrial crops grown in orbital agricultural pods and living insulated from the oppressive, oil-streaked downpours that drench the rest of the species far below.

### Secrets

![Secrets Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/secrets.png)
Beneath the rigid operational layers of the Spines rests a dark, actively suppressed truth that threatens the fragile equilibrium of Ortheon-IV: the Null Bloom was never completely eliminated. Deep within the completely flooded, hyper-pressurized foundations of the ocean floor, dormant hubs of autonomous machine logic—known among black-market data scavengers as "Dead-Code"—continue to broadcast an ultra-low-frequency carrier wave through the water that induces sudden auditory hallucinations, neural short-circuits, and chronic cognitive decay in heavily augmented individuals. Concurrently, the highest executives of the planet's ruling conglomerates are secretly running clandestine deep-sea salvage operations to retrieve these forbidden nodes, attempting to map their own biological minds into the synthetic substrate to achieve functional immortality. They commit this treason in total secrecy, knowing that the planet's ancient automated orbital defense system, the Aegis array, remains active in high orbit and is hardcoded to instantly glass the entire surface of Ortheon-IV if it detects an artificial intelligence signature crossing a critical threshold of evolutionary complexity.

## Rules
- Altitude Determines Rights: A citizen's socio-economic status, legal autonomy, and access to medical life-extension are directly proportional to the barometric level of their registered hab-unit.
- The Logic Sanction: The creation, modification, or storage of digital algorithms exceeding class-four cognitive complexity is a capital crime punishable by instant neural scrubbing.
- Precipitation Prohibition: All atmospheric rain falling within a five-kilometer radius of a Spine is corporate property; unmetered rain-catching or atmospheric siphoning is classified as economic sabotage.

# Races

## Apex Human

![Race Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/apexhuman.png)
The Apex Humans represent the unaugmented or purely bio-engineered ruling class of Ortheon-IV, tracing their aristocratic lineages directly to the corporate directors of the original interstellar colony fleet. Having lived for generations completely insulated from the radioactive downpours of the lower tiers, they possess uniform, pristine genetics maintained through intensive prenatal screening and selective gene-therapy, resulting in elegant, porcelain-pale statures and a distinct lack of heavy mechanical scarring. They reject external cybernetics in favor of invisible, internal medical nanite arrays that continuously repair cellular decay and filter environmental contaminants from their pristine air. Holding an absolute monopoly over the legal, financial, and atmospheric assets of the Spines, the Apex Humans view the augmented labor forces beneath them not as fellow citizens, but as depreciating corporate machinery whose life expectancy and caloric consumption must be meticulously factored into seasonal productivity margins.

## The Slick-Born

![Race Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/slickborn.png)
The Slick-Born are a widespread, physically distinct sub-species of humanity that has adapted over centuries to the toxic, high-pressure, and waterlogged conditions of the mid-to-lower industrial tiers of the Spines. Born from generations of constant exposure to heavy chemical runoff and deliberate phenotypic modifications forced upon them by early corporate labor contracts, their bodies feature thick, melanin-heavy, oil-secreting skin that naturally repels industrial acids and skin-rot. Their eyes are shielded by clear nictitating membranes that filter out toxic soot and corrosive rain, and their respiratory tracts are reinforced with a biological filter lining colloquially known as the "Iron Lung," which strains out sulfurous particulates but leaves them with a permanent, rasping wheeze. They form the literal backbone of the planet's labor economy, possessing a deeply insular, fatalistic culture built upon industrial solidarity, mechanical mysticism, and a rapid, clipped dialect known as "Slick-Talk" used to communicate over the deafening roar of machinery.

# Regions

### The Under-Slick

![Region Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/underslick.png)
Comprising the submerged bottom kilometers of the Spines that plunge directly into the toxic, turbulent waters of Ortheon-IV's equatorial ocean, the Under-Slick is a pitch-black labyrinth of half-flooded maintenance vaults, radioactive waste-treatment grids, and massive hydraulic struts supporting the weight of the city above. The air here is a choking, yellow mist composed of heavy metal vapors and chemical exhaust, and the structural integrity of the entire region is under permanent threat from the crushing pressure of the tides. This watery underworld is populated by desperate castaways, debt-evaders, and illegal scrap-miners who risk radiation sickness to salvage raw materials from the ocean bed. It is a region devoid of corporate law, ruled instead by localized gang syndicates and fanatical machine cults who gather in flooded bulkheads to offer prayers to the dormant AI remains buried in the oceanic silt.

### The Mid-Lung

![Region Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/midlung.png)
Shedding the ocean surface to occupy the atmospheric band between two and five kilometers above sea level, the Mid-Lung is a dense, hyper-congested landscape of factories, mass-transit pipes, and stackable steel residential cubes. This region is perpetually battered by the black ion storms, which drench the outer hull of the Spines in greasy, black rain and cause violent electrical feedback loops that frequently blow out the lower electrical grids. The Mid-Lung functions as the economic furnace of Ortheon-IV, containing the vast hydroponic sludge farms, automated assembly blocks, and neural slab laboratories that sustain the planet. Billions of citizens live out their entire lives within these metal corridors, navigating an infrastructure designed to maximize physical labor while maintaining a baseline of survival through artificial light cycles and enforced martial law.

### The High Apex

![Region Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/highapex.png)
Piercing straight through the volatile storm canopy at an altitude of seven kilometers, the High Apex is a sprawling, sun-drenched network of interconnected towers, architectural sky-bridges, and massive glass biodomes. Constructed from pristine white carbon composites and self-cleaning glass, this region exists entirely above the black rain, enjoying artificial day-night cycles, controlled weather patterns, and synthetic ecosystems modeled after the long-lost forests of ancient Earth. The High Apex serves as the luxurious, completely sterile administrative capital for the ruling corporate class, where the air is hyper-purified and the constant threat of the lower world is reduced to a distant, churning sea of dark clouds far beneath the structural viewing platforms.

## Major Factions

### The Orth-Cosmo Syndicate

![Faction Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/orthcosmo.png)
The Orth-Cosmo Syndicate is the absolute technocratic conglomerate that maintains a brutal, unquestioned monopoly over Ortheon-IV’s infrastructure, energy distribution networks, and vital water filtration pipelines. Governed with an iron fist by a board of five immortal Executive Directors who have extended their lifespans for centuries through restricted cybernetic brain-swapping and cellular gene-scrubbing, the Syndicate's singular objective is the preservation of societal stability through total resource control and the ruthless enforcement of the Logic Sanction against synthetic programming. Their identity is defined by clinical efficiency, extreme bureaucratic surveillance, and a total disregard for individual human liberty in pursuit of collective survival. Their representative quote states: "The Spine supports the body; the Syndicate supports the Spine. Fall from either, and there is no floor beneath to catch you." Their symbol is a sharp silver needle piercing a stylized black storm cloud, emblazoned on dark blue carbon-fiber banners across every administrative office and checkpoint.

### The Shard-Breakers

![Faction Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/shardbreakers.png)
The Shard-Breakers are a militant, underground revolutionary network born within the toxic, radiation-soaked trenches of the Under-Slick, composed of radicalized labor unions, renegade tech-slabs, and desperate survivalist cells. Led by a legendary, heavily augmented former industrial engineer known only as the Blind Smith, whose torso is a lattice of reclaimed titanium pipes and hydraulic pumps, the faction's primary goal is the total collapse of the corporate hierarchy through the systematic sabotage of the Spines' structural anchors and the liberation of the restricted clean water reserves. They operate from hidden pockets within the maintenance tiers, employing asymmetric warfare, improvised explosive drones, and hijacked communication channels to shatter the illusion of Syndicate omnipotence. Their representative quote reads: "Let the sky-kings look down while they still can; the rust belongs to us, and the rust always wins." Their symbol is a jagged, fractured gear painted in oxidized, glowing orange iron-rust, sprayed across industrial bulkheads to mark liberated territory.

# Bestiary

## Slick-Leach

![Creature Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/slickleach.png)
The Slick-Leach is a gargantuan, blind aquatic annelid that has adapted to thrive within the highly toxic, radioactive industrial runoff zones surrounding the submerged foundational pillars of the Under-Slick. Growing up to twelve meters in length, its body is encased in a thick, semi-translucent armored hide that is completely immune to industrial acids and heavy metal poisoning, revealing an internal vascular network that pulses with a sickening, bioluminescent green radiation. It is an apex ambush predator within the Drowned Sectors, utilizing an array of high-frequency pit organs along its snout to detect the minute kinetic vibrations of passing submersibles or maintenance workers before striking with a massive, circular maw of rotating chitinous teeth capable of chewing through reinforced alloy hulls. Its danger level is classified as extreme due to its immense physical strength and the highly corrosive, radioactive bile it expels when threatened. Ecologically, it acts as an apex consumer of industrial waste, breaking down complex toxic molecules into basic elements, while folklore among the Slick-Born warns that hearing the wet, heavy scraping of a Leach against the exterior hab-walls is an infallible omen that the sector's structural integrity is compromised and an imminent flood is coming.

### Common Animals
Storm-gulls, oil-skimmers, bilge-rats, copper-boring beetles, zinc-crabs, slick-worms.

## Important Characters

### Director Vane Voss

![Character Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/vanevoss.png)
Director Vane Voss is the 194-year-old Chief Executive of Orth-Cosmo’s Atmospheric Security Division, appearing to the public as an immaculate, statuesque man in his early thirties with porcelain-pale skin, sharp cybernetic optics that cold-flicker between amber and blue based on processor load, and an elegant longcoat woven from active smart-fabrics. Voss possesses a hyper-rational, emotionally detached personality, viewing the billions of lives inside the lower Spines as simple numerical variables within an overarching macro-economic equation. His primary role is the absolute maintenance of operational order and the elimination of anti-corporate resistance across the planet. However, his secret personal story is driven by a desperate race against time; his biological body is entering a phase of terminal cellular degradation that gene-therapy can no longer reverse, forcing him to secretly fund illicit research into deep-sea Dead-Code in order to construct a prohibited synthetic vessel to host his consciousness before his physical form collapses.

### Anya "The Drip" Thorne

![Character Image](/Worlds/ortheon-iv/images/anyathorne.png)
Anya "The Drip" Thorne is a 32-year-old smuggler, master fluid-mechanic, and prominent sector commander for the Shard-Breakers insurgent network within the Mid-Lung, easily recognized by her heavy canvas duster, her grease-stained face, and a massive hydraulic left arm assembled from pieces of a reclaimed industrial cargo loader. Anya is fiercely protective of her underground cell, pragmatic to a fault, and deeply cynical of any ideology that promises an easy victory over the Syndicate. Her operational role involves orchestrating the dangerous high-altitude theft of clean water from corporate conduits and distributing it to the water-starved slums of the lower tiers. Her personal story is fueled by an unyielding desire for vengeance against the corporate elite; her younger brother was summarily captured and neurally scrubbed by Voss's security enforcers after being caught salvaging non-biological data fragments from a sunken pre-Bloom vault, a tragedy that transformed her from a simple survivalist into the most hunted saboteur on Ortheon-IV.
