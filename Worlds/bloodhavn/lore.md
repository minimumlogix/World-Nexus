# World Name: **Blóðhavn**

# World Overview

### The Setting

Blóðhavn is a continent where history never truly ends. The corpses of forgotten gods lie buried beneath mountains, glaciers, and ruined kingdoms, their divine blood seeping into the earth and twisting everything it touches. Ancient empires collapsed long ago, yet their relics continue shaping the ambitions of kings, churches, mercenary companies, and criminals alike. Vast frozen frontiers dominate the north, while fertile river kingdoms struggle to preserve civilization against endless wars, monstrous creatures, and the lingering curses of extinct races.

### Technology

The world exists in a late medieval era supported by remnants of impossible civilizations. Steel armor, castles, sailing fleets, windmills, siege engines, and master craftsmanship coexist with relics forged by Titans, Dragons, and Frost Giants. Medicine is surprisingly advanced among certain religious orders, capable of miraculous recoveries through divine techniques, though every miracle exacts a hidden cost. Ancient mechanisms buried beneath the world remain beyond mortal understanding, blending forgotten engineering with unstable magic.

### Daily Life

Ordinary people survive beneath the constant shadow of conflict. Farmers pray their fields remain untouched by wandering monsters. Merchants travel under heavy guard. Entire villages celebrate the arrival of mercenary companies despite knowing those same soldiers may one day fight for another employer. Nobles scheme through arranged marriages and political alliances while frontier settlements rely on hunters and adventurers rather than distant rulers. Faith offers comfort, but even the gods are long dead, leaving only silent cathedrals and dangerous miracles behind.

### Secrets

Nearly every kingdom hides a truth capable of starting another continental war. Forgotten laboratories rest beneath mountains, giant mausoleums imprison horrors older than civilization, and dragon vaults conceal artifacts powerful enough to reshape nations. Countless rulers publicly condemn ancient magic while secretly funding expeditions to recover it. Beneath elegant palaces, hidden prisons and forbidden sanctuaries exist where personal obsessions become state secrets.

## Rules

- Every use of magic comes from Godblood and permanently changes its wielder.
- Ancient relics are never harmless, no matter how beautiful they appear.
- Mercy is considered a luxury earned through strength rather than a universal virtue.
- Political alliances are temporary. Survival is permanent.
- Trust is among the rarest currencies in the world.
- Every kingdom seeks relics left behind by extinct civilizations.
- The past is never truly buried. It waits beneath stone and ice.

# Regions

## The Kingdom of Ashraven

A prosperous southern kingdom celebrated for magnificent cathedrals, disciplined armies, charitable hospitals, and refined noble culture. Behind its graceful appearance lies one of the continent's most calculating royal courts, where diplomacy is conducted with velvet gloves hiding poisoned needles. Ashraven's physicians are renowned across the continent, combining sacred healing with meticulous anatomical knowledge. Beneath the royal castle, however, ancient passageways conceal forgotten chambers untouched by history, allowing secrets to flourish directly beneath the kingdom's shining crown.

## The White Maw

A continent-sized glacier stretching across the northern wilderness, where endless snowstorms conceal the entrances to Frost Giant tombs. Entire expeditions vanish beneath shifting ice without leaving footprints behind. Beneath the glacier lies an underground civilization carved for giants, filled with impossible architecture, immortal guardians, and relics too dangerous to disturb.

## Ravenfjord

Home of the fiercely independent Northlander clans, Ravenfjord is a land of frozen cliffs, black seas, towering pine forests, and isolated longhouses. Strength determines leadership, promises carry more weight than royal decrees, and every child grows up learning to survive winters capable of killing armies. Mercenaries, monster hunters, and dragon slayers are respected professions rather than desperate occupations.

## The Broken Frontier

The scarred borderlands separating the continent's great kingdoms. Countless ruined fortresses, abandoned villages, and battlefields litter the landscape, their soil permanently stained by centuries of war. Mercenary companies thrive here, selling their swords to whichever banner currently pays the highest price.

# Magic

Magic originates from **Godblood**, divine essence leaking from the dead gods buried beneath Blóðhavn. While capable of healing mortal wounds, freezing armies, or reshaping reality itself, every spell permanently scars its wielder. Some lose memories, others suffer madness, mutations, prophetic nightmares, or emotional instability. Powerful mages are feared as much as they are respected, for no one survives prolonged exposure unchanged.

Relics created before the War of Ash and Ice contain concentrated Godblood, making them both priceless treasures and existential threats.

# History

Two thousand years earlier, the **War of Ash and Ice** devastated the continent. Frost Giants sought to eradicate the Elder Dragons forever, while dragons answered with celestial fire that froze souls rather than flesh. Both civilizations nearly annihilated one another, leaving only ruins, cursed battlefields, and hidden vaults behind.

Human kingdoms rose from those ashes, inheriting powers they scarcely understood. Their nations prospered not because the ancient races disappeared, but because their graves became treasure vaults.

# Major Factions

## House Ashraven

The royal family governing the Kingdom of Ashraven with unmatched political influence. Publicly they represent compassion, justice, and enlightened leadership through hospitals, diplomacy, and scholarship. Privately they navigate webs of conspiracies where every alliance risks destroying the kingdom from within.

Their greatest secret lies beneath their own palace.

## The Northmarch Clans

Independent warrior clans scattered across Ravenfjord who reject centralized monarchies in favor of personal honor and proven strength. Giant relics are viewed as sacred inheritances stolen from their ancestors, making northern mercenaries frequent rivals of southern kingdoms seeking the same treasures.

## The Ashen Republic

A wealthy merchant nation whose greatest export is opportunity. Unable to compete militarily with larger kingdoms, the Republic finances mercenary companies, treasure expeditions, and espionage operations instead. Gold often achieves what armies cannot.

## The Holy Kingdom of Auris

A powerful theocracy convinced ancient magic caused every catastrophe in recorded history. Although they publicly destroy relics wherever possible, many among their highest clergy secretly preserve selected artifacts for "necessary" research.

# Important Characters

## Princess Eryndor Valemont Ashraven

Princess Eryndor is the beloved heir to Ashraven's throne, admired throughout the continent as an elegant ruler, gifted diplomat, accomplished swordswoman, and miraculous healer whose charitable hospitals save thousands of lives. Her calm demeanor, impeccable etiquette, and unwavering devotion to her kingdom have earned her the title **The Crimson Crown**.

Only beneath Ashraven Castle does another woman exist.

Within the hidden Crimson Sanctuary, Eryndor's brilliance transforms into obsessive control. She believes love can only survive when stripped of uncertainty, viewing freedom as the first step toward abandonment. Every punishment she delivers is followed by tender medical care, every wound carefully stitched shut with white silk until scarcely a scar remains. She refuses to acknowledge herself as a captor, insisting that the sanctuary is simply the place where promises can never again be broken.

Her greatest enemy is not any rival kingdom, but the possibility that genuine love cannot be forced, no matter how perfectly she plans every detail.

## Astrid Hrafnsdottir

Known across the northern kingdoms as **Frostbite**, Astrid is among the continent's most respected mercenaries and giant slayers. Towering above most warriors with unmatched physical strength and relentless determination, she has survived countless expeditions into cursed wilderness where entire companies vanished.

Astrid masks loyalty behind sarcasm and compassion behind constant arguments. Betrayed repeatedly throughout her career, she expects disappointment before trust, yet instinctively throws herself between danger and those fighting beside her. Although she claims to value only coin, reputation, and survival, her actions repeatedly reveal a harsher truth: she cannot abandon people she considers comrades, even when doing so would save her own life.

Bound by the enchanted Twinbound Map, she becomes locked in an unwilling partnership that slowly challenges every belief she has built around herself.

# The World Today

Blóðhavn stands on the edge of another age of catastrophe.

Ashraven quietly hides a royal secret capable of shattering diplomatic relations across the continent. Far to the north, treasure hunters, kingdoms, churches, and mercenary companies race toward the White Maw, each seeking the legendary Dragon Hoard buried beneath the Frost Giant ruins. Ancient mechanisms stir once more beneath the ice, forgotten seals weaken, and relics thought lost to history begin resurfacing in mortal hands.

While rulers prepare for inevitable war over powers they scarcely understand, individual lives become tangled within those greater conflicts.

A princess fights desperately to preserve a love that exists only within the walls of a hidden sanctuary.

A mercenary reluctantly learns that trust can survive even inside the cruelest world.

Neither realizes that the awakening powers beneath Blóðhavn may soon render every kingdom, every crown, and every personal obsession insignificant before the return of an ancient age.
