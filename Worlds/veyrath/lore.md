# Veyrath's Lore

## World Overview

- **The Setting**: Veyrath is a cold, fractured continent where towering walled cities rise like islands above a sea of famine, plague, and violence. Every valley belongs to a banner lord, every road demands tribute, and every noble lineage claims descent from ancient blood-sorcerers who once bargained with celestial entities during the forgotten Age of Red Crowns. The continent is divided into dozens of city-states, marcher duchies, and unstable petty kingdoms constantly locked in cycles of siege, arranged marriages, assassinations, and religious disputes. Though knights and heraldry dominate appearances, the true currency of power is inherited magical blood. A noble without magic is vulnerable; a commoner with magical talent is hunted, hidden, or quietly executed. The emotional atmosphere of Veyrath is one of decaying grandeur: candlelit courts hiding starvation beyond their walls, sacred vows masking paranoia, and ancient castles built atop graves that still whisper.
- **Technology**: Veyrath rests technologically between the late medieval and early renaissance eras. Windmills, fortified aqueducts, steel plate armor, manuscript presses, siege bombards, and primitive navigational devices exist, though unevenly distributed. Most technology stagnates because noble mage-families suppress innovations that might weaken their monopoly over magical labor. Enchantments replace many practical industries: mage-lit lanterns illuminate noble districts, weather-charms protect vineyards, and blood-sealed mills grind grain year-round. However, these services are ruinously expensive. Common villages rely on oxen, candlelight, and hand-forged tools. Roads are poorly maintained outside wealthy regions, making long-distance travel dangerous. Guild engineers secretly experiment with blackpowder weapons and mechanical devices, but most discoveries vanish into aristocratic vaults before reaching the masses.
- **Daily Life**: Life depends entirely on birth. Nobles live in towering keeps filled with velvet banners, poison-tasters, scribes, tutors, and elaborate political rituals. Young aristocrats are tested constantly for magical inheritance, and marriages are negotiated like military alliances to preserve blood potency. Merchant guilds occupy an uneasy middle class, wealthy enough to challenge lords economically but legally inferior to mage-blooded families. Guild districts bustle with debtors, caravan masters, smugglers, and contract killers. Meanwhile, serfs endure relentless hardship. Villages suffer crop failures, roaming mercenaries, predatory tax collectors, and monster raids from untamed wilderness regions. Entire communities vanish during winter famines while nearby courts host masked festivals celebrating dynastic anniversaries. Religion offers little comfort; most temples openly serve noble patrons first and the poor second.
- **Secrets**: The noble bloodlines are not truly hereditary in the way people believe. Ancient records hidden beneath ruined monasteries reveal that bloodline magic originally came from parasitic astral entities known as the Veinborn, beings that fused themselves into human dynasties centuries ago. Noble families unknowingly pass fragments of these entities through their blood. The stronger the magic, the less human the lineage becomes over generations. Some ancient houses have already transformed into monstrous things hidden beneath glamours and ceremonial masks. The secretive cult threatening the continent, known as the Hollow Saints, has discovered a horrifying truth: dormant magical potential exists in nearly all humans, deliberately suppressed long ago through ritual mass-sacrifice to ensure noble dominance. The cult believes awakening this dormant blood will destroy the aristocracy forever, even if civilization itself burns in the process.

## Rules

1. Bloodline magic weakens drastically when noble blood mixes with common ancestry, making noble marriage politics brutally important.
2. Killing a mage allows rare rituals to extract remnants of their magical essence from the blood and bones.
3. Serfs cannot legally leave noble lands without written permission sealed by a blood-marked authority.
4. Guilds may not possess military forces larger than fifty armed retainers without noble approval.
5. Open use of unregistered magic is punishable by execution under the Covenant Laws.

## Races

- **Humans**: Humans dominate Veyrath politically and numerically. Noble bloodlines obsess over ancestry, preserving vast genealogical archives and arranging strategic marriages to maintain magical potency. Common humans endure crushing inequality but possess remarkable resilience and adaptability. Human societies range from brutal militaristic duchies to decadent republics ruled indirectly by merchant councils. Most humans secretly resent the mage nobility, though fear prevents rebellion. Humans serve every role imaginable in the continent, from armored knights and plague doctors to peasant rebels and cult infiltrators.
- **Vaelkin**: The Vaelkin are pale, long-lived mountain peoples descended from failed noble blood experiments centuries ago. Their silver-veined skin and glowing pupils reveal traces of unstable magical mutation. Feared as cursed half-breeds, they are often segregated into isolated fortress settlements high in the Frostspine ranges. Vaelkin possess heightened resistance to blood magic and are highly sought after as bodyguards and mage hunters. Though physically frail, they possess exceptional memory and oral traditions preserving histories erased elsewhere. Many secretly aid the Hollow Saints.
- **Drenori**: The Drenori inhabit marshlands and drowned coastal ruins along the southern continent. Broad-shouldered and amphibious-featured, they are descendants of sailors altered by ancient sea rituals during the collapse of the Red Crown Empire. Drenori culture values communal survival over hierarchy, making them deeply distrusted by nobles. They dominate black-market shipping routes, smuggling forbidden texts, escaped serfs, and magical relics between city-states. Their oral songs contain encoded maps to submerged ruins filled with forbidden blood relics.
- **Hollowborn**: The Hollowborn are not a natural race but humans whose dormant bloodline potential awakened catastrophically without proper noble conditioning. Their veins glow beneath the skin, their emotions distort reality nearby, and prolonged stress mutates their bodies unpredictably. Some become miracle workers or prophetic visionaries; others transform into violent aberrations. Most are hunted immediately by authorities or recruited by the Hollow Saints. Rumors claim entire hidden villages of Hollowborn exist beneath ruined cities.

## Regions

- **The Ashen Marches**: A scorched frontier of ruined castles, abandoned villages, and endless battlefields where petty lords wage perpetual wars over exhausted farmland. Mercenary companies thrive here, switching allegiance constantly. The dead rarely stay buried due to lingering blood magic saturating the soil.
- **Kingdom of Valedorn**: The wealthiest kingdom in Veyrath, famous for its ivory palaces, elaborate blood courts, and ruthless dynastic politics. Nearly every major noble house traces lineage to Valedorn’s ancient imperial bloodlines. Public executions here are treated as social spectacles.
- **The Black Fen**: A vast diseased marshland ruled unofficially by smugglers, exiles, escaped serfs, and hidden cult networks. Entire villages drift on floating reed platforms. Strange lights and whispers lure travelers into drowned ruins beneath the bog waters.
- **Frostspine Dominion**: A mountainous territory controlled by isolated fortress-cities where the Vaelkin endure persecution and military conscription. Massive monasteries carved into cliffs preserve forbidden historical archives guarded by blind warrior-monks.
- **The Shattered Coast**: Once a thriving imperial shoreline, now broken by earthquakes and magical catastrophes. Half-submerged towers rise from stormy waters while pirate kingdoms and Drenori clans fight over ancient relic vaults hidden beneath flooded cities.
- **The Golden Republic of Serathis**: A wealthy merchant republic outwardly governed by guild councils but secretly manipulated by banking dynasties tied to noble bloodlines. Assassinations, economic sabotage, and espionage dominate politics here more than open warfare.

## Major Factions

- **The Crimson Thrones**: The collective alliance of major noble bloodlines attempting to preserve aristocratic rule across Veyrath. Leadership rotates between dominant houses through ritual conclaves held every seven years. Their symbol is a crown pierced by thirteen crimson veins stitched into black silk banners. Publicly celebrated as protectors of civilization, they secretly maintain breeding programs to strengthen magical bloodlines. **Representative Quote:** “The lowborn fear our rule because they cannot imagine the chaos without it.”
- **The Hollow Saints**: A clandestine revolutionary cult seeking to awaken dormant blood magic among the masses and destroy hereditary mage rule forever. Their leader, the faceless prophet known only as Saint Calvein, allegedly survived noble blood extraction rituals as a child. Their symbol is an empty circle painted in ash and blood. Publicly they are condemned as terrorists and heretics, though starving peasants increasingly shelter them. **Representative Quote:** “If power lives in blood, then all blood must burn equally.”
- **The Iron Ledger Consortium**: A coalition of merchant guilds, bankers, and caravan syndicates attempting to weaken noble dominance through economic control. Officially they deny political ambitions, but secretly they fund mercenaries and forbidden technological research. Their sigil is a chained scale surrounding a golden coin. **Representative Quote:** “Gold survives every dynasty.”
- **The Veiled Synod**: A secretive religious order embedded throughout temples and noble courts alike. They claim to preserve spiritual balance, but in truth they guard knowledge about the Veinborn entities and suppress investigations into the true origin of blood magic. Their members wear featureless silver masks during private ceremonies. Their symbol is a closed eye beneath a halo of thorns. **Representative Quote:** “Truth is a plague civilization cannot survive.”
- **The Brotherhood of Saint Iron**: A militant knightly order composed mostly of lowborn soldiers granted limited authority by desperate rulers. Publicly loyal to the kingdoms, many members secretly sympathize with commoner uprisings. Their banners depict an iron sword wrapped in chains. **Representative Quote:** “A starving man owes no lord obedience.”

## Bestiary

- **Vein Wraiths**: Ghostlike entities formed when powerful blood mages die violently without proper rites. Vein Wraiths appear as translucent figures connected by floating strands of glowing crimson tissue. They haunt battlefields and ruined keeps, draining warmth and sanity from nearby humans. Nobles fear them intensely because they often reveal hidden family crimes before killing victims.
- **Carrion Stags**: Towering skeletal deer-like creatures roaming plague-ridden forests. Black fungal growths pulse beneath their exposed ribs, releasing toxic spores when threatened. Peasants believe seeing one foretells famine or civil war. Despite their terrifying appearance, their blood is used in rare healing tonics.
- **Mire Sirens**: Amphibious predators inhabiting the Black Fen. They mimic human voices perfectly to lure travelers into deep waters before drowning them. Drenori communities sometimes domesticate juvenile Mire Sirens as guardians.
- **Cathedral Worms**: Massive pale subterranean creatures living beneath ancient cities and cathedrals. Their movements destabilize foundations and uncover buried crypts. Some scholars believe they feed specifically on magical blood residue beneath noble strongholds.
- **Crown Ravens**: Large black birds with unnaturally intelligent behavior. They gather near executions and battlefields, often repeating overheard phrases in distorted whispers. Many suspect they serve the Veiled Synod as spies.
- **Common animals**: Oxen, plague dogs, marsh eels, black goats, pale warhorses, carrion crows, frost wolves, ash boars, tower cats, river drakes.
